  CHALLENGE</span></h4>\r\n<p>Excess nutrients (nitrogen and phosphorus) in the nation&rsquo;s
  waters is one of the costliest and most challenging environmental problems we face.&nbsp;
  Over the last 50 years, the amount of nutrients entering into our waters has escalated,
  and is now one of the top causes of water quality impairment. Protecting human health
  and the environment from adverse impacts of excessive nutrients is a national priority.</p>\r\n<p>Many
  organizations and communities are interested in making use of sensors to provide
  improved spatial and temporal data that can help inform decisions and actions to
  protect and restore our nation&rsquo;s water resources.</p>\r\n<p>This Challenge

  and resulting data to improve decisions and actions resulting in more effective
  nutrient management.</p>\r\n<hr />\r\n<h4 style=\"text-align: justify;\"><span style=\"color:

  interested in deploying nutrient sensors to address an important nutrient-related
  water quality issue.</p>\r\n<p>Organizations should be currently engaged in water
  quality monitoring and have some level of experience using sensors, managing data,
  and communicating that information.</p>\r\n<p style=\"text-align: justify;\"><strong>Participation
