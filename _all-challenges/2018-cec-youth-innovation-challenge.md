description: 'Welcome to the 2018 Commission for Environmental Cooperation (CEC) Youth
  Innovation Challenge! This challenge aims to engage North American youth in the
  work of the CEC, increase awareness of shared priority sustainability issues among
  Canada, Mexico and the United States, and facilitate communication and collaboration
  between North American youth on entrepreneurship, innovation and partnerships for
  green growth.    North American youth aged 18 to 30 are invited to <a href="https://cec.ideascale.com/">enter
  the challenge</a> to compete for C$5,000 in seed funding and a chance to present
  their idea to North America’s top environmental officials at the 2018 CEC Council
  Session in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma.    From all the ideas on the platform, nine
  semifinalists will be invited to develop their innovative ideas into full proposals.  <p
  style="font-weight: 400;">Please keep in mind that there are two phases for this
  competition.</p>    <ul>    <li style="font-weight: 400;">The Idea Phase runs from Jan.
  15 to April 3.</li>    <li style="font-weight: 400;">The Proposal Phase begins April
  16 and has a deadline for submissions on May 7.</li>  </ul>  <p style="font-weight:
  400;">To be eligible for the Proposal Phase, it must first be submitted in the Idea
  Phase. Be sure to read the <a href="https://cec.ideascale.com/a/pages/guidelines">full
  guidelines for participation</a>.</p>  Make sure to tell us about your green idea
  on the Youth Innovation Challenge <a href="https://www.facebook.com/CECYouth4Innovation">Facebook
  page</a> or Twitter using the #Youth4Innovation hashtag! Follow the CEC on Facebook
  at <a href="http://www.facebook.com/CECconnect">www.facebook.com/CECconnect</a> or
  on <a href="https://twitter.com/CECweb">Twitter at https://twitter.com/CECweb</a>.    <a
  href="http://www.cec.org/how-green-is-your-dream">Click here</a> for the Semifinalist
  Project Summaries and Winners of the 2017 Youth Innovation Challenge.    [embed]https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uSqil3RIces[/embed]    <strong>About
  the CEC</strong>    The Commission for Environmental Cooperation (CEC) was established
  by the governments of Canada, Mexico and the United States through the North American
  Agreement on Environmental Cooperation, the environmental side agreement to NAFTA.
  An intergovernmental organization, the CEC brings together citizens and experts
  from governments, nongovernmental organizations, academia and the business sector
  to seek solutions to protect North America’s shared environment while supporting
  sustainable economic development. Find out more at: <a href="http://www.cec.org/">www.cec.org</a>    CEC
  initiatives are undertaken with the financial support of the Government of Canada,
  through the Federal Department of Environment; the Government of the United States
  of America, through the Environmental Protection Agency; and the Government of the
  United States of Mexico, through the Secretaría de Medio Ambiente y Recursos Naturales.'
